Brooklyn Horror Film Festival
Until Oct 18 2018., Dive deep into the terrors and artistic triumphs of genre filmmaking at this annual festival, which features premieres of new scary movies like the grisly satire Cannibal Club, the shorts anthology The Field Guide to Evil and the documentary Wolfman's Got Nards. Head to “Miskatonic: Big Scares on the Small Screen,” for a discussion on made-for-TV horror movies, and don't miss the cursed ’70s flick ATRUM, which is showing alongside a documentary about the film's history of ill-fated screenings. On second thought, maybe do miss that.

Trevor Powers
Music, Pop. Williamsburg. Oct 17 2018. PHOTO: CHRIS SCHOONOVER Trevor Powers is the Boise, ID–based singer, songwriter and producer formally known as Youth Lagoon. He retired that moniker in 2015 and has since been studying classical theory and ancient modes, making music inspired by the works of visual artists like Francis Bacon and Sister Gertrude Morgan and travelling through Europe, Asia and the U.S. writing poetry. The fruits of those endeavors have thus far presented themselves as entrancing glitch-pop tunes brimming with unstable experimental textures. It's hard to think of a better venue to house them than National Sawdust.

Big Thief
Music, Rock and indie. Big Thief. Williamsburg. Oct 17 2018. Big Thief’s second full-length release, Capacity, appeared last year and found guitarist and vocalist Adrianne Lenker following up her folk-rock band's critically acclaimed debut, Masterpiece, with a slow-burner that expertly distilled years of songwriting experience into a wide-ranging yet seamless collection. You'll hear from it at this show, and be mighty glad you did.

Black Light
Photograph: Courtesy Joan Marcus. Theater, Musicals. Black Light. West Village. Until Dec 31 2018. Daniel Alexander Jones (Duat) inhabits his longtime alter ego, Jomama Jones—or does she inhabit him?—in a high-concept musical evening that reflects on a shattered mirror of black history. Jomama is a paradigm of R&B-diva grandeur circa 1982, with impeccable posture and elocution that bespeak an old-school black-star dignity. Although the original "Afromystical" songs don’t always rise to the occasion, it’s a pleasure to bask in Jones’s sequined, oracular presence, especially when Jones allows us to see the pain and labor behind the all-but-impervious diva’s self-fashioning.

Addie Weyrich
Comedy. Addie Weyrich: Only Child Syndrome. Park Slope. Until Oct 15 2018. Loveable loony toon Addie Weyrich looks back on the bizarro-world behaviors of a single child home at her charming new comedy roundup. She invites some of her favorite stand-ups and performers, including Liza Treyger, Andrew Coalson, Monroe Martin and X Mayo, to go into their own familial mishegas at the first edition.

Matt Rogers, Bowen Yang, Zach Zimmerman and Larry Owens
Comedy. QueerEyeRL. Gowanus. Oct 18 2018. In early 2018, the world was given the darling bounty of Antoni, Jonathan, Karamo, Tan and Billy—Netflix’s Queer Eye Fab Five. Well, it looks like Brooklyn has inherited its own iconic quintet. The unsolicited cultural critics and endlessly funny nutcases Zach Zimmerman, Pat Regan, Larry Owens, Matt Rogers and Bowen Yang are your fearsome five at this soon-to-be-legendary parody show. For who better to advise on food and wine, design, clothing, grooming and culture than a pack of broke Brooklyn comedians? One brave audience member will be nominated for an onstage makeover, from which there is likely no return. You can even nominate your friends for a questionable lifestyle redux, which will likely get more deranged as the night continues. Perhaps their shenanigans will impress the original pantheon, just in time for Antoni and Jonathan to take on New York Comedy Festival? We truly can’t wait to see how this goes down.

Vintage Basement with Max and Nicky
Comedy, Stand-up. Vintage Basement with Max & Nicky. East Village. Until Nov 19 2018. Twin brothers and vaudeville aficionados Max and Nicky Weinbach bring class back to the comedy scene at their monthly affair, at which stand-ups and musical performers dress up and serve their best sets.

Yoga Unwined
Things to do, Classes and workshops. Yoga Unwined. Greenpoint. Oct 17 2018-Oct 21 2018. This hour-long, vinyasa-based class pairs creative yoga poses with wine facts for a truly enlightening experience. The session concludes with an optional sparkling wine tasting so you can really get into the flow. Who said peace of mind can't come with a little buzz?

Jarek Luk
Things to do. Sound Bath: "Healing Vibrations" with Jarek Luk. Bedford-Stuyvesant. Oct 17 2018. Take shelter from the sturm and drang of the city at this sonic meditation session, which triggers inner healing with gongs, singing bowls, zaphir chimes and magic quartz singing pyramids. This old-school (like, ancient) artform helps restore healthy respiratory and heart rates, reduce anxiety and stimulate a meditative state. Even if crystals and gongs make you want to call an exorcist, you're certain to find simple, commitment-free peace at this expert session. And besides, all you have to do is lie down and chill out. What's not to love?

Tammy Faye Starlite
Music, Cabaret and standards. Tammy Faye: She Comes in Colors. East Village. Oct 18 2018-Oct 25 2018. The daring, hilarious, persona-shifting singer, who has previously taken on the oeuvres of Nico and Marianne Faithfull, blasted through the rock & roll canon of Mick Jagger and Keith Richards in her fall concert series at Pangea last year, devoting a show apiece to the Rolling Stones albums Beggars Banquet, Let It Bleed, Sticky Fingers and Exile on Main Street. Now she returns to cover the Stones' trippy 1967 venture Their Satanic Majesties Request.

Things to do, Exhibitions. "Germ City: Microbes and the Metropolis". East Harlem. Until Apr 28 2019. Germs, bacteria, viruses—New Yorkers encounter them by the millions every day while going about their business. Just thinking about what could be covering those poles in subway cars is enough to make your skin crawl. But contagious bugs have always been part of city life, and in this exhibition, the Museum of the City of New York charts the history of diseases in Gotham, and the many strategies that municipal authorities employed to combat them.

St. Patrick's Old Cathedral Things to do, Walks and tours. Catacombs by Candlelight Tour. Nolita. Until Dec 31 2018 This exclusive tour beneath St. Patrick’s Basilica takes you through the storied underground resting places of legendary New Yorkers including bishops and Civil War generals.

Harry Potter: A History of Magic Things to do. "Harry Potter: A History of Magic". Upper West Side. Oct 16 2018-Jan 26 2019. PHOTO: COURTESY JIM KAY, BLOOMSBURY PUB. Dive into the ancestors of the world’s most-beloved magical saga at this spectacular exhibition, which collects artifacts from the British Library, the New-York Historical Society and J.K. Rowling’s own archives. You’ll learn about the history of dragons, griffins and other essentials of Hogwarts lore, peer at rare notes and art from Rowling and illustrator Mary GrandPré, and view costumes from the current production of Harry Potter and the Cursed Child. Brace yourself for a museum gift shop more packed than Honeydukes.

Get there early.

Haus of Sweat: Bodyroll Things to do, Sport events. Haus of Sweat Bodyroll. Greenpoint. Until Oct 29 2018. Music Venues

Manhattan

(le) poisson rouge. This music venue has attracted classic and indie rock acts to its Greenwich Village basement, featuring a sweet sound system and modular stage, since 2008. 158 Bleecker Street (between Thompson & Sullivan Streets), Manhattan, NY, lpr.com

Beacon Theatre. Recently renovated yet still gaudily-gilded, 2,894-seat historic three-tiered theater, hosts large touring lvive music performances on the UWS. 2124 Broadway, Manhattan, NY, 10023, msg.com/beacon-theatre/

Birdland. Classic Theater District jazz club with roomy red environs, a large bar and Cajun-flavored food, also boasts the new basement performance space Birdland Theater. 315 W. 44th Street (between 8th & 9th Avenues), Manhattan, NY, birdlandjazz.com

Blue Note. International renowned Greenwich Village jazz club, with locations in Japan, Slovakia and Italy, features big bands and American food in a dinner theater venue. 131 W 3rd St, Manhattan, NY, 10012, bluenotejazz.com/newyork/

Bowery Ballroom. The Lower East Side 575-person capacity mid-size music club provides an intimate room with great sound to view up-and-coming musicians and touring acts. 6 Delancey Street (between Bowery & Chrystie Street), Manhattan, NY’, boweryballroom.com

Carnegie Hall. Practice, practice, practice is how you get to this storied Midtown concert venue, hosting every genre from chamber recitals to rock concerts. 881 7th Avenue (between W 56th & W. 57th Streets), Manhattan, NY, carnegiehall.org

David Geffen Hall, Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts. The classical concert venue, formerly Avery Fisher Hall, at Lincoln Center features a 2,738-seat auditorium opened in 1962, and is home to the New York Philharmonic. 10 Lincoln Center Plaza, Manhattan, NY, nyphil.org

Hulu Theater at Madison Square Garden, 4 Penn Plaza. This more intimate version of MSG, seats 2,000 and 5,600 for concerts, comedy shows and stage performances. 7th Avenue (between 31st & 33rd Streets), Manhattan, NY, theateratmsg.com

Irving Plaza. A ballroom-style live music venue in the Union Square area attracts rock and rap acts to the stage of the 1200-capacity music club. 17 Irving Place (between 15ht & 16th Street), Manhattan, NY irvingplaza.com

Lincoln Center, Frederick P. Rose Hall, the Appel Room. Jazz at Lincoln Center presents jazz, opera, theater and dance programs in an amphitheater offering stunning views of Columbus Circle as a backdrop. Broadway (60th Street), Manhattan, NY, AmericanSongbook.org.

Madison Square Garden, 4 Penn Plaza. The gargantuan 20,000-seat Garden supports local sports teams, like the Knicks, Liberty and Rangers, besides concerts from touring music bigwigs. 7th Avenue (between 31st & 33rd Streets), Manhattan, NY, thegarden.com

Mercury Lounge. The intimate 250-capacity rock music venue and bar attracts indie headliners, up-and-coming local bands, and even launched the Strokes. 217 E Houston St, Manhattan, NY, 10002, mercuryloungenyc.com

Metropolitan Opera, Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts. The largest classical music company in the U.S., the Metropolitan Opera presents approximately 27 operas each season in the legendary opera house at Lincoln Center. 30 Lincoln Center Plaza (between 63rd & 64th Streets), Manhattan, NY, metopera.org

Radio City Music Hall. The massive 6000-seat midtown Manhattan Art Deco theater plays host to the Rockettes and their Christmas Spectacular, as well as high kicking rock royalty too. 1260 6th Ave, Manhattan, NY, 10020, radiocity.com

Terminal 5. The Bowery Presents massive 3000-person capacity multi-tiered concert hall attracts big names rock and indie rock acts to its Westside warehouse setting. 610 56th Street (between 11th & 12th Avenues), Hell’s Kitchen, Manhattan, NY, terminal5nyc.com.

The Frick Collection. The UES mansion turned museum’s music series, debuted in 1938, features classical keyboard recitals, chamber groups, and early music ensembles, by internationally acclaimed musicians. 1 E. 70th Street (5th Avenue), Manhattan, NY, frick.org.

Village Vanguard. Historic West Village basement jazz club, formerly featuring cool cats like John Coltrane and Miles Davis, continues to boast the best in mainstream jazz. 178 7th Ave S, Manhattan, NY, 10014, villagevanguard.com

Forest Hills Stadium. Renovated 14,000-seat outdoor tennis stadium and live music venues plays host to huge concerts, from historic shows by the Beatles and Stones to Paul Simon’s tour finale. 1 Tennis Pl, Forest Hills, Brooklyn, NY, 11375, foresthillsstadium.com

Kings Theatre. Opened in 1929, the ornate 3,074-seat theater was reborn in 2015 after a $95 million restoration, attracting famous comedians and musicians to Flatbush. 1027 Flatbush Avenue (Tilden Avenue), Brooklyn, NY, kingstheatre.com

Output. Multi-level Williamsburg dance club with great sound system attracting big-name electronics DJs to separate rooms and rooftop. 74 Wythe Avenue (North 12th Street), Williamsburg, Brooklyn, NY, 11211, outputclub.com

Saint Vitus. Darkly lit Greenpoint club plays host to louds bands playing metal, misfit and experimental live music. 1120 Manhattan Ave, Brooklyn, NY, 11222, saintvitusbar.com
